## Direct answer

A hotel or ryokan hiring under the SSW accommodation field must verify that the actual role falls within the official field scope and that the business meets current field requirements. It should then confirm the candidate's accepted accommodation skill and Japanese-language evidence, offer direct and appropriate employment, and prepare the SSW1 support plan before the residence filing.

## Hiring table

| Area | Check |
| --- | --- |
| Duties | Front desk, planning/public relations, hospitality, restaurant service and permitted related work |
| Business | Accommodation authorization, field procedures and compliant employment |
| Candidate | Skill and Japanese tests or an accepted exemption |
| Contract | Direct employment, pay, hours, site, housing and deductions |
| Support | Pre-entry, arrival, housing, orientation, consultation and language learning |

Do not design a role that consists only of an unrelated or merely incidental task. Prepare multilingual guest communication, emergency instructions, night-shift handover and workplace mentoring. Build the start date backward from test evidence, residence processing, housing and training rather than from peak-season demand alone.

## FAQ

### Can the worker be hired only for room cleaning?

The complete role must fit the official accommodation activities and permitted related duties. Check the latest operating guideline, not a single task label.

### Must the hotel provide support itself?

It may provide qualified support directly or entrust it, while retaining oversight. See the [accommodation visa guide](/en/visa/tokutei-ginou-shukuhaku).
