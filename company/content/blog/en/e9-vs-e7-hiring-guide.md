## Direct answer

E-9 is a non-professional employment route administered through Korea's Employment Permit System. E-7 covers designated activities and is divided into categories whose occupation, candidate and employer requirements differ. Neither route should be selected from a job title alone: the employer must map the actual duties, workplace, candidate evidence and company conditions to the current official rules.

## Five-point comparison

| Check | E-9 focus | E-7 focus |
| --- | --- | --- |
| Structure | EPS allocation and permit procedure | Designated activity and occupation |
| Job | Eligible industry and workplace | Duties matching an E-7 occupation |
| Candidate | EPS roster and entry process | Education, career, qualification and pay by category |
| Employer | Domestic recruitment effort and quota | Company, contract and category requirements |
| Timing | Permit round and allocation | Evidence review and immigration filing |

Write a detailed job description before recruiting. Record the work site, tasks, skills, shift pattern, pay components and reporting line. For E-7, compare that description with the exact occupation and candidate evidence. For E-9, confirm the current eligible industry, employer headcount and permit round. Contract terms and actual workplace practice must remain consistent after entry.

## FAQ

### Can an employer use E-7 simply because a candidate has experience?

No. The designated occupation, duties, candidate evidence, pay and employer requirements must all be checked.

### Which route is faster?

There is no universal answer. E-9 depends on EPS rounds and allocation; E-7 timing depends on the category and evidence. Start with the [E-9 guide](/en/visa/e-9) and [E-7 guide](/en/visa/e-7), then verify current notices.
