## Direct answer

To hire a Nepali candidate under SSW1, a Japanese employer must first confirm that the real duties fall within an SSW field. It must verify the candidate's accepted skill and Japanese-language evidence, make a compliant employment contract, prepare the SSW1 support plan and file the applicable residence procedure. Nepal-related departure steps and Japan's immigration process should be placed on the same schedule but treated as distinct requirements.

## Employer sequence

1. Fix the field, work category, duties, work site and direct-employment structure.
2. Verify tests or any officially accepted exemption from the candidate's history.
3. Explain pay, hours, deductions, housing and support in a language the candidate understands.
4. Prepare applicant, accepting-organization, field and support-plan documents separately.
5. File the Certificate of Eligibility or other applicable residence procedure.
6. Complete pre-departure, arrival, housing, orientation, consultation and reporting tasks.

Japan's official Nepal guidance says use of a sending organization is not mandatory for SSW procedures. If one is used, document its role, fees and evidence responsibility. Outsourcing support to a registered support organization also does not erase the accepting organization's employment and oversight obligations.

## FAQ

### Is a sending organization mandatory for Nepali SSW candidates?

No, not under the Japanese official Nepal guidance. Check any current Nepal-side requirements for the individual case.

### Does a support organization take all employer responsibility?

No. The employer must maintain lawful employment, required filings and oversight. See the [SSW overview](/en/visa/tokutei-ginou) and the current field rules.
